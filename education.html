📚 Multilevel Cropping Strategies

Natural farming models mimic native forest layers. Instead of cultivating a single crop (monoculture), farmers stack compatible plants with different heights and root depths on the same plot. This maximizes sunlight capture, naturally repels pests, and enriches soil organic matter.

Click on any layer below to expand details about companion seeds and ecosystem benefits.
🌳 Layer 1: Canopy / Tall Timber
Height: 30+ feet
Representative Crops: Coconut, Arecanut, Teak, Neem, Tamarind.

Ecological Function: Acts as the top shield. Captures intense direct sunlight, protects smaller crops from heavy wind and rain damage, and anchors the deep water table. Neem trees also act as natural pest repellents for the entire field.

Root System: Deep anchor roots that extract moisture from deep soil layers, leaving upper soil nutrients for shallower crops.
🌴 Layer 2: Understory / Medium Fruit
Height: 15 - 30 feet
Representative Crops: Banana, Papaya, Mango, Moringa, Citrus.

Ecological Function: Thrives in filtered sunlight. Banana plants store vast quantities of water in their pseudostems, cooling the microclimate. Moringa leaves decompose into high-nitrogen organic mulch.

Root System: Lateral branching roots in the mid-soil layers.
🌿 Layer 3: Shrub & Bush
Height: 6 - 15 feet
Representative Crops: Coffee, Cocoa, Black Pepper (climber on arecanut), Lemon, Curry Leaves.

Ecological Function: Partial shade-loving crops. They populate the middle air space. Black pepper vines use the trunks of canopy trees (Arecanut/Coconut) as supports, saving space.

Root System: Medium density fibrous roots.
🌱 Layer 4: Ground Cover & Herbaceous
Height: 1 - 6 feet
Representative Crops: Chili, Cowpea (legume), Maize, Marigold, Beans, Leafy Greens.

Ecological Function: Cover crops protect the soil from direct baking by the sun, reducing water evaporation by 60%. Legumes (cowpea, beans) fix atmospheric nitrogen into the soil. Marigolds act as trap crops, attracting pests away from cash crops.

Root System: Shallow fibrous root networks.
🥔 Layer 5: Rhizosphere / Root Zone
Depth: Below ground
Representative Crops: Turmeric, Ginger, Sweet Potato, Cassava, Garlic.

Ecological Function: Underground storage rhizomes. They loosen soil density naturally. Garlic acts as a soil-borne fungicide and nematocide. Turmeric and ginger have high market value and require dark, moist soil under the canopy shade.

Root System: Dense storage tubers feeding off the rich upper humus layer.
